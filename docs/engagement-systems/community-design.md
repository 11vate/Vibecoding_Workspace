# Community Design

## Purpose

Design of community systems that foster connection, sharing, and long-term engagement through social interaction.

**Community creates retention and engagement. This document enables community design.**

---

## Community Elements

### Social Features

**Features**:
- User profiles
- Friend systems
- Messaging
- Notifications

**Why**: Social features enable connection.

---

### Sharing Systems

**Systems**:
- Content sharing
- Achievement sharing
- Progress sharing
- Creation sharing

**Why**: Sharing enables expression and connection.

---

### Collaborative Activities

**Activities**:
- Group challenges
- Team goals
- Collaborative creation
- Shared experiences

**Why**: Collaboration creates connection.

---

### Competitive Systems

**Systems**:
- Leaderboards
- Rankings
- Competitions
- Comparisons

**Why**: Competition creates engagement.

## Community Design Principles

### 1. Enable Connection

**Principle**: Make it easy to connect.

**Application**: Simple friend systems, clear profiles, easy messaging.

**Why**: Connection is foundation of community.

---

### 2. Facilitate Sharing

**Principle**: Make sharing easy and rewarding.

**Application**: One-click sharing, sharing rewards, sharing visibility.

**Why**: Sharing creates engagement and discovery.

---

### 3. Support Collaboration

**Principle**: Enable collaborative activities.

**Application**: Group features, team systems, collaborative tools.

**Why**: Collaboration creates connection.

---

### 4. Enable Competition

**Principle**: Provide competitive opportunities.

**Application**: Leaderboards, rankings, competitions, comparisons.

**Why**: Competition creates engagement.

---

### 5. Foster Positive Community

**Principle**: Create positive community culture.

**Application**: Moderation, positive reinforcement, community guidelines.

**Why**: Positive community increases retention.

---

## Community Types

### Casual Community

**Type**: Light social interaction.

**Characteristics**:
- Simple features
- Low commitment
- Easy participation
- Broad appeal

**Use Case**: General audiences, low barrier.

---

### Engaged Community

**Type**: Active social interaction.

**Characteristics**:
- Rich features
- High commitment
- Active participation
- Deep engagement

**Use Case**: Dedicated users, high engagement.

---

### Creative Community

**Type**: Creation and sharing focus.

**Characteristics**:
- Creation tools
- Sharing systems
- Showcase features
- Recognition systems

**Use Case**: Creative expression, user-generated content.

## How Cursor Uses Community Design

### When Designing Systems

Cursor must:
1. **Design community elements** - Social, sharing, collaboration, competition
2. **Apply community principles** - Connection, sharing, collaboration, competition, positivity
3. **Choose community type** - Casual, engaged, creative
4. **Test community effectiveness** - Measure engagement, adjust

**Never design systems without community design.**

---

### When Evaluating Designs

Cursor must ask:
- Are community elements present?
- Are community principles applied?
- Is community type appropriate?
- Is community effective?

**Never accept designs without community design.**
