# Design Questions Checklist

## Purpose

Comprehensive checklist of design questions Cursor must answer before designing, ensuring all design dimensions are considered.

**Systematic questions force thorough thinking. This checklist ensures completeness.**

---

## User Understanding Questions

### Who Are Users?

**Questions**:
- Who is the target audience?
- What are their demographics?
- What are their psychographics?
- What are their needs?

**Why**: Understanding users enables user-centered design.

---

### What Do Users Want?

**Questions**:
- What are user goals?
- What are user desires?
- What problems do users have?
- What motivates users?

**Why**: Understanding wants enables goal-aligned design.

---

### How Do Users Think?

**Questions**:
- What are user mental models?
- How do users process information?
- What are user cognitive limits?
- How do users learn?

**Why**: Understanding thinking enables cognitive-aligned design.

---

## Experience Design Questions

### What Is the Core Fantasy?

**Questions**:
- What fantasy does this fulfill?
- What does the user believe they are doing?
- What emotional contract exists?
- What must this never become?

**Why**: Core fantasy drives experience design.

---

### What Are the Emotional Beats?

**Questions**:
- What discovery moments exist?
- What tension moments exist?
- What mastery moments exist?
- What reward moments exist?

**Why**: Emotional beats create engagement.

---

### What Is the Core Loop?

**Questions**:
- What is the basic action cycle?
- How does the loop work?
- What makes users repeat?
- How does the loop evolve?

**Why**: Core loop is the heart of experience.

---

## System Design Questions

### What Systems Are Needed?

**Questions**:
- What systems support the experience?
- How do systems interact?
- What are system dependencies?
- How do systems scale?

**Why**: Systems enable experience.

---

### How Do Systems Evolve?

**Questions**:
- How do systems start?
- How do systems grow?
- What unlocks when?
- How does complexity increase?

**Why**: Evolution maintains engagement.

---

### What Are System Boundaries?

**Questions**:
- What is each system responsible for?
- What are system inputs/outputs?
- What are system dependencies?
- What are system failure modes?

**Why**: Boundaries prevent confusion.

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Use Checklist

**Use checklist when**:
- Starting new projects
- Designing new features
- Evaluating designs
- Auditing systems

**Why**: Checklist ensures completeness.

---

### How to Use Checklist

**Usage process**:
1. Go through each category
2. Answer all relevant questions
3. Document answers
4. Reference answers in design
5. Update answers as design evolves

**Why**: Systematic usage ensures thoroughness.
