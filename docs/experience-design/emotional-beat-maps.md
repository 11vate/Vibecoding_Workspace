# Emotional Beat Maps

## Purpose

Visual mapping of emotional beats throughout the experience, ensuring emotional moments are designed intentionally.

**Emotional beat maps ensure emotional experiences are planned, not accidental.**

---

## Beat Map Structure

### Beat Types

**Discovery Beats**:
- Finding something new
- Learning something interesting
- Uncovering secrets
- Experiencing novelty

**Tension Beats**:
- Facing challenges
- Making difficult decisions
- Experiencing uncertainty
- Taking risks

**Mastery Beats**:
- Demonstrating skill
- Optimizing performance
- Achieving goals
- Receiving recognition

**Reward Beats**:
- Receiving rewards
- Completing goals
- Unlocking content
- Achieving milestones

**Recovery Beats**:
- Recovering from failure
- Reducing stress
- Resting
- Reflecting

---

### Beat Timing

**When Beats Occur**:
- **Early**: Discovery, learning
- **Mid**: Tension, mastery
- **Late**: Achievement, recognition
- **Throughout**: Rewards, recovery

**Why**: Timing creates emotional rhythm.

## Beat Map Creation

### Step 1: Identify Beat Opportunities

**What to Do**:
- List all user actions
- Identify emotional potential
- Map to beat types
- Prioritize beats

**Output**: Beat opportunity list

---

### Step 2: Design Beat Moments

**What to Do**:
- Design specific beat moments
- Create beat experiences
- Plan beat timing
- Ensure beat variety

**Output**: Beat moment designs

---

### Step 3: Map Beat Flow

**What to Do**:
- Map beat sequence
- Plan beat timing
- Ensure beat variety
- Create beat rhythm

**Output**: Beat flow map

---

### Step 4: Validate Beat Map

**What to Do**:
- Test beat effectiveness
- Validate emotional impact
- Ensure beat variety
- Refine beat timing

**Output**: Validated beat map

## Beat Map Patterns

### Discovery-Heavy

**Pattern**: Many discovery beats.

**Use Case**: Exploration-focused experiences.

**Characteristics**:
- Frequent discoveries
- Exploration rewards
- Hidden content
- Novel interactions

**Why**: Discovery-heavy creates exploration engagement.

---

### Tension-Heavy

**Pattern**: Many tension beats.

**Use Case**: Challenge-focused experiences.

**Characteristics**:
- Frequent challenges
- Risk/reward decisions
- High stakes
- Skill testing

**Why**: Tension-heavy creates challenge engagement.

---

### Balanced

**Pattern**: Balanced beat types.

**Use Case**: General experiences.

**Characteristics**:
- Variety of beats
- Balanced rhythm
- Multiple engagement types
- Broad appeal

**Why**: Balanced creates broad engagement.

## How Cursor Uses Beat Maps

### When Designing Experiences

Cursor must:
1. **Create beat map** - Identify and design beats
2. **Plan beat timing** - When beats occur
3. **Ensure beat variety** - Different beat types
4. **Validate beat effectiveness** - Test emotional impact

**Never design experiences without emotional beat maps.**

---

### When Evaluating Designs

Cursor must ask:
- Are emotional beats mapped?
- Is beat timing appropriate?
- Is there beat variety?
- Are beats effective?

**Never accept designs without emotional beat maps.**
