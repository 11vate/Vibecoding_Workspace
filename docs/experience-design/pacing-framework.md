# Pacing Framework

## Purpose

Comprehensive framework for designing experience pacing, ensuring engagement through well-paced emotional and mechanical progression.

**Proper pacing creates engaging experiences. Poor pacing creates boredom or overwhelm.**

---

## Pacing Dimensions

### Temporal Pacing

**What It Controls**: Time-based progression.

**Elements**:
- Session length
- Activity duration
- Wait times
- Cooldown periods

**Design Considerations**:
- Appropriate session length
- Varied activity durations
- Reasonable wait times
- Meaningful cooldowns

**Why**: Temporal pacing controls time experience.

---

### Mechanical Pacing

**What It Controls**: Mechanic introduction and complexity.

**Elements**:
- Mechanic introduction rate
- Complexity increase
- System unlocking
- Feature availability

**Design Considerations**:
- Gradual mechanic introduction
- Appropriate complexity increase
- Meaningful unlocks
- Balanced feature availability

**Why**: Mechanical pacing controls complexity experience.

---

### Emotional Pacing

**What It Controls**: Emotional intensity and variety.

**Elements**:
- Emotional intensity
- Tension curves
- Reward frequency
- Recovery periods

**Design Considerations**:
- Varied emotional intensity
- Well-designed tension curves
- Appropriate reward frequency
- Adequate recovery periods

**Why**: Emotional pacing controls emotional experience.

---

### Progression Pacing

**What It Controls**: Advancement and growth.

**Elements**:
- Progress rate
- Milestone frequency
- Achievement pacing
- Mastery progression

**Design Considerations**:
- Satisfying progress rate
- Meaningful milestones
- Appropriate achievement pacing
- Balanced mastery progression

**Why**: Progression pacing controls advancement experience.

---

## Pacing Patterns

### Slow Build

**Pattern**: Gradual, steady increase.

**Use Case**: Long-term engagement, mastery focus.

**Characteristics**:
- Slow start
- Gradual increase
- Steady progression
- Long-term focus

**Why**: Slow build creates mastery and depth.

---

### Fast Start

**Pattern**: Quick initial engagement, then steady.

**Use Case**: Immediate engagement, retention focus.

**Characteristics**:
- Quick start
- Immediate engagement
- Then steady pace
- Retention focus

**Why**: Fast start creates immediate engagement.

---

### Variable Pacing

**Pattern**: Alternating fast and slow periods.

**Use Case**: Maintaining interest, preventing monotony.

**Characteristics**:
- Alternating pace
- Prevents monotony
- Maintains interest
- Creates variety

**Why**: Variable pacing prevents boredom.

---

## Pacing Principles

### 1. Start Strong

**Principle**: First impressions matter.

**Application**: Create strong initial experience.

**Why**: Strong start creates engagement.

---

### 2. Maintain Momentum

**Principle**: Keep engagement going.

**Application**: Provide continuous value.

**Why**: Momentum maintains engagement.

---

### 3. Create Variety

**Principle**: Vary pacing to prevent monotony.

**Application**: Alternate fast and slow.

**Why**: Variety prevents boredom.

---

### 4. Allow Recovery

**Principle**: Provide rest periods.

**Application**: Include low-intensity periods.

**Why**: Recovery prevents burnout.

---

### 5. Build to Peaks

**Principle**: Create peak moments.

**Application**: Build tension to peaks.

**Why**: Peaks create memorable moments.

## How Cursor Uses This Framework

### When Designing Experiences

Cursor must:
1. **Design temporal pacing** - Session length, activity duration
2. **Design mechanical pacing** - Mechanic introduction, complexity
3. **Design emotional pacing** - Intensity, tension, rewards
4. **Design progression pacing** - Progress rate, milestones
5. **Apply pacing principles** - Start strong, maintain momentum, create variety

**Never design experiences without pacing framework.**

---

### When Evaluating Designs

Cursor must ask:
- Is pacing appropriate?
- Is there variety?
- Are peaks created?
- Is recovery allowed?

**Never accept designs without proper pacing.**

Pacing framework succeeds when:

- ✅ Peaks are created
- ✅ Users stay engaged
