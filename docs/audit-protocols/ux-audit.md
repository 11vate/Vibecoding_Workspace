# UX Audit Protocol

## Purpose

Systematic protocol for auditing UX quality, ensuring interfaces are usable, accessible, and emotionally resonant.

**UX audits prevent usability issues and ensure quality experiences.**

---

## Audit Scope

### What to Audit

**UX Elements**:
- Usability
- Accessibility
- Cognitive load
- Emotional design
- Consistency
- Feedback

**Why**: Comprehensive audit ensures UX quality.

---

## Audit Process

### Step 1: Usability Audit

**What to Check**:
- Is interface usable?
- Are tasks completable?
- Is navigation clear?
- Are interactions intuitive?

**Output**: Usability audit results

---

### Step 2: Accessibility Audit

**What to Check**:
- Is interface accessible?
- Are keyboard shortcuts available?
- Is screen reader support adequate?
- Are ARIA labels present?

**Output**: Accessibility audit results

---

### Step 3: Cognitive Load Audit

**What to Check**:
- Is cognitive load manageable?
- Is information organized?
- Are choices limited?
- Is learning supported?

**Output**: Cognitive load audit results

---

### Step 4: Emotional Design Audit

**What to Check**:
- Does interface create positive emotions?
- Is feedback satisfying?
- Are interactions delightful?
- Does it create meaning?

**Output**: Emotional design audit results

---

### Step 5: Consistency Audit

**What to Check**:
- Is design consistent?
- Are patterns reused?
- Is language consistent?
- Are interactions consistent?

**Output**: Consistency audit results

---

### Step 6: Feedback Audit

**What to Check**:
- Is feedback immediate?
- Is feedback clear?
- Are loading states present?
- Are error messages helpful?

**Output**: Feedback audit results

---

### Step 7: Synthesis

**What to Do**:
- Synthesize audit findings
- Identify issues
- Prioritize problems
- Recommend fixes

**Output**: Audit report with recommendations

---
