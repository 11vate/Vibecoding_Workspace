# Behavioral Patterns Research

## Purpose

Research on behavioral patterns and decision-making biases, grounding UX decisions in behavioral psychology.

**This research grounds UX in behavioral psychology.**

---

## Key Behavioral Patterns

### Loss Aversion

**Pattern**: Losses feel twice as bad as equivalent gains.

**Research Findings**:
- Loss aversion is strong
- Framing affects perception
- Scarcity increases value
- Risk avoidance is powerful

**Design Implications**:
- Frame rewards as avoiding loss
- Create scarcity
- Emphasize risk of inaction
- Use loss framing carefully

**Why**: Loss aversion is a powerful motivator.

---

### Anchoring

**Pattern**: First information influences subsequent decisions.

**Research Findings**:
- Anchors are powerful
- Reference points matter
- Comparisons influence perception
- Context affects judgment

**Design Implications**:
- Set appropriate anchors
- Use reference points
- Show comparisons
- Establish value context

**Why**: Anchoring influences perception and decisions.

---

### Social Proof

**Pattern**: People follow what others do.

**Research Findings**:
- Social proof is influential
- Popularity matters
- Community activity drives behavior
- Testimonials are effective

**Design Implications**:
- Show what others are doing
- Display popularity
- Highlight community activity
- Use testimonials

**Why**: Social proof influences behavior.

---

### Scarcity

**Pattern**: Scarcity increases perceived value.

**Research Findings**:
- Scarcity creates desire
- Limited availability increases value
- Time limits create urgency
- Exclusivity is appealing

**Design Implications**:
- Create legitimate scarcity
- Show limited availability
- Use time limits
- Highlight exclusivity

**Why**: Scarcity increases desire and urgency.
